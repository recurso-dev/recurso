**Effective Date:** June 23, 2026

## 1. Introduction

Recurso ("we," "us," "our"), operated through [recurso.dev](https://recurso.dev), is a billing engine platform that enables businesses to manage subscriptions, invoicing, payments, and revenue operations. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect information when you use our platform, API, dashboard, documentation, and related services (collectively, the "Service").

This Privacy Policy applies to:

- **Account Holders:** Businesses and individuals who create a Recurso account.
- **End Users:** Individuals whose data is processed through the Service by our Account Holders.
- **Website Visitors:** Individuals who visit our website without creating an account.

By accessing or using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Account Information

When you create a Recurso account, we collect:

- **Identity Information:** Name, email address, company name, job title, and phone number.
- **Authentication Information:** Password (stored in hashed form), multi-factor authentication data, and single sign-on (SSO) provider identifiers.
- **Billing Information:** Payment method details, billing address, tax identification numbers (such as GSTIN, VAT ID), and transaction history for your Recurso subscription.
- **Organizational Information:** Team member details, roles, and permissions configured within your account.

### 2.2 Customer Billing Data (Processed on Your Behalf)

When you use Recurso to manage billing for your end users, we process on your behalf:

- **End User Identity Data:** Names, email addresses, and other identifiers you configure for your customers.
- **Subscription Data:** Plan selections, subscription status, billing cycles, and entitlements.
- **Payment Data:** Payment method tokens (we do not store raw credit card numbers; tokenization is handled by payment processors), transaction records, refund history, and payment status.
- **Invoice Data:** Invoice line items, amounts, tax calculations, currency information, and invoice delivery records.
- **Usage and Metering Data:** Consumption records, feature usage metrics, and metered billing events that you submit through the API.

### 2.3 Usage Analytics

We automatically collect information about how you interact with the Service:

- **Service Usage Data:** API call logs (endpoints accessed, request/response metadata, timestamps, and status codes), dashboard interactions, feature usage patterns, and configuration changes.
- **Device and Browser Information:** IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device type, screen resolution, and language preferences.
- **Performance Data:** Page load times, error rates, latency measurements, and other technical performance metrics.
- **Referral Data:** Referring URLs, search terms, and campaign parameters that brought you to our website.

### 2.4 Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use cookies, local storage, and similar tracking technologies to:

- **Essential Cookies:** Maintain your session, authenticate your identity, and ensure security. These cannot be disabled.
- **Functional Cookies:** Remember your preferences, language settings, and dashboard configurations.
- **Analytics Cookies:** Understand how visitors interact with our website and Service to improve the user experience. We use privacy-respecting analytics tools.
- **Marketing Cookies:** With your consent, deliver relevant advertising and measure campaign effectiveness. These are only used on our marketing website, never within the application dashboard.

For more details, see Section 12 (Cookie Policy).

### 2.5 Communications Data

We collect information from communications with us, including:

- Support ticket content, chat transcripts, and email correspondence.
- Survey responses and feedback submissions.
- Community forum posts and interactions.

## 4. Data Processing Role

### 4.1 Recurso as Data Processor

With respect to **Customer Billing Data** (i.e., the personal data of your end users that you process through the Service), Recurso acts as a **data processor** (or "service provider" under CCPA, or "data fiduciary processing on behalf of another data fiduciary" under India's DPDPA).

In this capacity:

- We process Customer Billing Data solely on your instructions and for the purpose of providing the Service.
- We do not sell, rent, or use Customer Billing Data for our own independent purposes.
- We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Customer Billing Data.
- We will assist you in fulfilling your obligations to respond to data subject requests.
- We will notify you without undue delay upon becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting Customer Billing Data.

**Your Responsibilities as Data Controller:** You are responsible for ensuring that you have a lawful basis for collecting and processing your end users' personal data, that appropriate privacy notices are provided to your end users, and that your use of Recurso complies with applicable data protection laws.

### 4.2 Recurso as Data Controller

With respect to **Account Information**, **Usage Analytics**, **Cookies Data**, and **Communications Data**, Recurso acts as an independent **data controller** (or "data fiduciary" under India's DPDPA).

In this capacity, we determine the purposes and means of processing this data as described in this Privacy Policy, and we are directly responsible for compliance with applicable data protection laws.

### 4.3 Data Processing Agreements

Customers on the Pro and Enterprise tiers may request a Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") that sets forth additional commitments regarding the processing of personal data. To request a DPA, contact [privacy@recurso.dev](mailto:privacy@recurso.dev).

## 8. Security Measures

### 8.1 Technical Measures

We implement the following technical security measures:

- **Encryption:** All data in transit is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher. Data at rest is encrypted using AES-256 or equivalent industry-standard encryption.
- **Access Controls:** Role-based access control (RBAC), multi-factor authentication (MFA), and the principle of least privilege are enforced for all access to systems containing personal data.
- **API Security:** API authentication via secure tokens, rate limiting, IP allowlisting (for Enterprise Customers), and request signing.
- **Infrastructure Security:** Network segmentation, firewalls, intrusion detection and prevention systems, and regular vulnerability scanning.
- **Audit Logging:** Comprehensive logging of access to and modification of personal data, with tamper-resistant log storage.

### 8.2 Organizational Measures

We implement the following organizational security measures:

- **Security Training:** Regular security awareness training for all employees with access to personal data.
- **Access Reviews:** Periodic reviews of access permissions and removal of access that is no longer required.
- **Incident Response:** Documented incident response procedures with defined escalation paths and notification timelines.
- **Vendor Assessment:** Security assessments of third-party vendors and subprocessors prior to engagement and on an ongoing basis.
- **Penetration Testing:** Regular penetration testing by qualified independent security assessors.

### 8.3 Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, we will:

- Notify affected Account Holders without undue delay, and in any event within seventy-two (72) hours of becoming aware of the breach.
- Provide details of the nature of the breach, the categories of data affected, the likely consequences, and the measures taken to address and mitigate the breach.
- Cooperate with affected Account Holders to fulfill their obligations to notify data protection authorities and data subjects, as required by applicable law.

## 9. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction and the applicable data protection laws, you may have certain rights regarding your personal data. Recurso is committed to honoring these rights in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA), and other applicable laws.

### 9.1 Rights Under GDPR (EEA, UK, and Switzerland)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights:

- **Right of Access:** You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
- **Right to Rectification:** You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
- **Right to Erasure ("Right to Be Forgotten"):** You have the right to request deletion of your personal data where there is no compelling reason for its continued processing.
- **Right to Restriction of Processing:** You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.
- **Right to Data Portability:** You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and to transmit it to another controller.
- **Right to Object:** You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data for direct marketing purposes or where processing is based on legitimate interests.
- **Right to Withdraw Consent:** Where processing is based on consent, you have the right to withdraw your consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing prior to withdrawal.
- **Right to Lodge a Complaint:** You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority in your jurisdiction.

**Legal Bases for Processing:** We process personal data under the following legal bases: (a) performance of a contract; (b) legitimate interests (such as improving the Service, ensuring security, and fraud prevention); (c) compliance with legal obligations; and (d) consent, where applicable.

### 9.2 Rights Under CCPA (California, USA)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the CCPA:

- **Right to Know:** You have the right to request information about the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the categories of sources, the business purposes for collection, and the categories of third parties with whom we share it.
- **Right to Delete:** You have the right to request deletion of personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions.
- **Right to Opt-Out of Sale:** You have the right to opt out of the sale of your personal information. As stated in Section 5.7, Recurso does not sell personal data.
- **Right to Non-Discrimination:** You have the right not to receive discriminatory treatment for exercising your CCPA rights.
- **Right to Correct:** You have the right to request correction of inaccurate personal information.

**Categories of Personal Information Collected:** Identifiers; commercial information; internet or electronic network activity information; professional or employment-related information; and inferences drawn from the foregoing.

### 9.3 Rights Under DPDPA (India)

If your personal data is processed under the jurisdiction of India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, you have the following rights:

- **Right to Access Information:** You have the right to obtain a summary of the personal data being processed and the processing activities undertaken.
- **Right to Correction and Erasure:** You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or misleading personal data, completion of incomplete personal data, updating of personal data, and erasure of personal data that is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected.
- **Right to Grievance Redressal:** You have the right to have a readily available means of grievance redressal provided by Recurso.
- **Right to Nominate:** You have the right to nominate another individual to exercise your rights in the event of your death or incapacity.

### 9.4 Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of the rights described above, you may:

- Use the self-service tools available in your account dashboard (for access, correction, export, and deletion).
- Submit a request to [privacy@recurso.dev](mailto:privacy@recurso.dev).
- Contact our Data Protection Officer (see Section 14).

We will respond to your request within the timeframe required by applicable law (generally thirty (30) days, extendable by an additional sixty (60) days for complex requests under GDPR).

### 9.5 Verification

To protect your privacy and security, we may need to verify your identity before fulfilling your request. Verification may include confirming your email address, account credentials, or other identifying information.

### 9.6 End User Rights

If you are an end user of one of our Account Holders and wish to exercise your data protection rights with respect to data processed through the Service, you should direct your request to the Account Holder (the data controller). We will assist our Account Holders in fulfilling their obligations to respond to such requests.

## 12. Cookie Policy

### 12.1 What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to enable website functionality, improve user experience, and provide analytics information.

### 12.2 Types of Cookies We Use

| Cookie Type | Purpose | Duration | Can Be Disabled |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Essential** | Authentication, session management, security, and load balancing | Session or up to 24 hours | No |
| **Functional** | Remembering preferences, language, and dashboard layout | Up to 12 months | Yes |
| **Analytics** | Understanding usage patterns, page views, and feature adoption | Up to 24 months | Yes |
| **Marketing** | Campaign attribution and advertising (marketing website only) | Up to 12 months | Yes |

### 12.3 Managing Cookies

You can manage your cookie preferences through:

- **Account Settings:** You can manage your cookie preferences in your account settings at any time.
- **Browser Settings:** You can configure your browser to block or delete cookies. Note that blocking essential cookies may impair the functionality of the Service.

### 12.4 Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are placed by third parties that provide services on our behalf (such as analytics providers). These cookies are subject to the respective third parties' privacy policies.
